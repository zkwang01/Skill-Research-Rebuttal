## Step 2: Respond to the Justification and Weaknesses

Recommended reading on rebuttal writing:

- [How We Write Rebuttals — Devi Parikh](https://deviparikh.medium.com/how-we-write-rebuttals-dc84742fece1)
- [Writing a Rebuttal for SIGGRAPH](https://research.siggraph.org/blog/guides/writing-a-rebuttal-for-siggraph/)

> **Rebuttal writing style:** Answer the reviewer's questions head-on. The reviewer should immediately know which of their questions you are addressing.
>
> Core principles:
> 1. **Give them what they ask for.** If the reviewer asks for something, provide it.
> 2. **Don't introduce new problems.** New claims in the rebuttal can easily become new attack surfaces.

## Step 3: Review and Verify Key Concerns

After finishing the first draft, mark each reviewer's key concerns (usually found in their justification). Repeatedly verify whether these concerns are properly addressed and whether the response is convincing enough to change the reviewer's mind. Have colleagues review the key responses as well.

> **Why focus on key concerns?** Each reviewer typically has only 1-2 core points they truly care about. Even if they write many paragraphs, they won't remember everything — but those 1-2 key points will stick with them.
>
> Of course, every point should be answered properly. But after drafting, spend most of your effort double-checking those key concerns, and ask colleagues to verify them too.
